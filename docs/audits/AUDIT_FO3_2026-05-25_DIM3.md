# Fallout 3 Compatibility Audit — 2026-05-25 (Dimension 3 only)

**Scope**: single-dimension run via `/audit-fo3 3` — focused audit of **ESM Record Coverage (Fallout3.esm)** against the shared FNV parser. The other 5 dimensions of the FO3 audit were not run today; see the **2026-05-24 full sweep** for current state of Dim 1, 2, 4, 5, 6.

## Executive Summary

`Fallout3.esm` (275 MB GOTY revision, HEDR 0.94) parses through the shared FNV parser in **~0.95 s release**, yielding **37,459 structured records across 83 categories** with **zero warnings at `RUST_LOG=trace`**. Every top-level GRUP label on disk is dispatched — no catch-all-skip leaks at the top level. The Session-12 expansion from 10 → 18 dispatched categories has effectively grown to **full top-level coverage**.

Three findings worth filing — all NEW (no matching open or closed issues in the 500 most recent):

| Severity | NEW | Carryover | Total | INFO |
|----------|-----|-----------|-------|------|
| HIGH     | 1   | 0         | 1     | —    |
| MEDIUM   | 1   | 0         | 1     | —    |
| LOW      | 1   | 0         | 1     | —    |

1. **HIGH — F-FO3-D3-01**: `WATR.NNAM` (FO3/FNV noise-texture path) silently dropped on 100% of FO3 WATRs (53/53) and FNV WATRs (78/78). Parser reads only `TNAM`, which Skyrim+ uses; FO3/FNV ship `NNAM` exclusively. Every FO3/FNV water surface ends up with empty `texture_path`.
2. **MEDIUM — F-FO3-D3-02**: `NAVM` records nested inside CELL persistent-children GRUPs are silently skipped at the cell walker's catch-all. Cross-game (FO3, FNV, Skyrim SE, FO4 all report `navmeshes=0`). Affects future AI/pathfinding only.
3. **LOW — F-FO3-D3-03**: `NPC_` / `CREA` `SCRI` script-attachment field not extracted. Quantified impact: **398 of 1,647 FO3 NPCs (24%)** and **148 of 533 creatures (27%)** drop their attached script. Gap is already documented at `index.rs:506-509`; this finding quantifies FO3 footprint for M47.0 sizing.

The brief's FNV baseline of "13,684 records" is the Session-12 figure; the live FNV baseline is **73,054 records** (the 60,000 floor at `crates/plugin/tests/parse_real_esm.rs:49`). Delta table below uses live numbers.

## FNV-Shared Surface (verified clean)

The FNV-shared parser carries the FO3 ESM load without per-game divergence on:

- **HEDR → GameKind dispatch**: GOTY 0.94 lands in `0.93..=0.95` band at `crates/plugin/src/esm/reader.rs:141-146`, mapping to `GameKind::Fallout3NV`. Pre-GOTY (0.85) falls through to the same kind.
- **CELL / XCLL / RCLR / XCLW layout**: identical to FNV (`cell/walkers.rs:246-316`, 40-byte-tail path lines 276-284, no per-game branch). Regression locked at `cell/tests/cell.rs:63-110`.
- **DIAL → INFO walker**: 6,381 DIAL records, 22,327 INFO children populated via `extract_dial_with_info` at `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/grup_walker.rs:105`. INFO conditions (CTDA) and scripts (SCHR/SCDA) remain deferred per `records/misc/ai.rs:290-292`.
- **SCPT pre-Papyrus bytecode**: 1,257 scripts on FO3; SCHR/SCDA/SCTX/SLSD/SCVR/SCRV/SCRO all populate. FO3-specific 20-byte SCHR tail vs Oblivion's 18-byte u16 is handled at `script.rs:128-138`.
- **TREE → SpeedTree**: 9 vanilla FO3 TREE bases all route through `has_speedtree_binary()` per `tests/parse_real_esm.rs:702-722`.
- **WATR DATA decode**: 53/53 FO3 WATRs decode to non-default fog/shallow colour. The DATA path is correct; only the NNAM texture-path arm is missing (F-FO3-D3-01).

## Findings

### F-FO3-D3-01 — WATR.NNAM (FO3/FNV noise-texture path) silently dropped

- **Severity**: HIGH
- **Status**: NEW (no matching open or closed issue — searched "WATR", "NNAM", "water noise")
- **Location**: `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/misc/water.rs:278-307`

**Description**

FO3 and FNV `WATR` records ship the noise/diffuse texture path in the **`NNAM`** sub-record (a zstring like `Data\Textures\Water\WastelandWaterPotomac.dds`). Skyrim+ uses `TNAM` for the same purpose. `parse_watr` reads only `TNAM`, so `WatrRecord.texture_path` is **empty on 100% of FO3 WATR records (0 of 53) and 100% of FNV WATRs (0 of 78)**.

**Evidence**

Sub-record histogram from raw EsmReader walk over `Fallout3.esm`:

```
FO3: WATR records = 53
  ANAM  53  (100%)
  DATA  53  (100%)
  DNAM  42  (79%)
  EDID  53  (100%)
  FNAM  53  (100%)
  FULL  38  (71%)
  GNAM  53  (100%)
  MNAM  53  (100%)
  NNAM  53  (100%)   <-- 100% of records ship NNAM
  SNAM  7   (13%)
  XNAM  51  (96%)
```

Sample decoded NNAM payloads:

| WATR EDID | NNAM (zstring) |
|---|---|
| `PotomacNRShallow` | `Data\Textures\Water\WastelandWaterPotomac.dds` |
| `DCMallWater` | `Data\Textures\Water\WastelandWaterPotomac.dds` |
| `WaterTypeMegatonWater` | `Data\Textures\Water\WaterFlowRippleNoise01.dds` |
| `WaterTypeOasisClean` | `Data\Textures\Water\WaterFlowRippleNoise01.dds` |
| `ReflectingPoolWaterType` | `""` (intentionally blank — reflecting pool) |

Zero records ship `TNAM` (`TNAM-bearing=0` from the WaterParams histogram).

**Impact**

Every FO3 and FNV water plane renders without its authored noise/displacement map. The cell-loader consumer at `byroredux/src/cell_loader/water.rs:286` reads `record.texture_path == ""` and falls through to either the per-water default (`textures/water/water_default.dds`) or — more likely — the magenta missing-texture placeholder for the `WaterPlane` material. Affects every FO3/FNV exterior water body: Potomac, Megaton water tower, Oasis, Rivet City, Reflecting Pool, plus FNV's Lake Mead, Hoover Dam, Quarry Junction ponds, the cazador swamps, every Vegas pool.

**Suggested fix**

In `parse_watr` at `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/misc/water.rs:284`, add an arm adjacent to the existing `TNAM` arm:

```rust
b"NNAM" => out.texture_path = read_zstring(&sub.data),
```

NNAM and TNAM are game-mutually-exclusive on Bethesda titles (FO3/FNV ship NNAM exclusively; Skyrim+ ships TNAM exclusively), so a last-arm-wins match is safe without a `GameKind` gate. Add a regression-test fixture using `PotomacNRShallow` as a clean candidate. Tests live at `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/misc/water.rs:321-449`.

### F-FO3-D3-02 — NAVM records inside cell children GRUPs silently skipped

- **Severity**: MEDIUM
- **Status**: NEW (no matching open or closed issue — searched "NAVM", "NAVMESH", "navigation")
- **Location**: `crates/plugin/src/esm/cell/walkers.rs:652-655`

**Description**

`parse_navm` exists at `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/misc/world.rs:47` and is wired into the top-level GRUP dispatcher (`records/mod.rs:433`). But the on-disk shape of NAVM is not a top-level GRUP — it lives **inside cell child-records GRUPs**:

```
GRUP CELL → CELL record → GRUP Cell Persistent Children → NAVM records
```

The cell walker's child-record loop has an explicit catch-all skip comment naming NAVM among the skipped types. As a result, `index.navmeshes.len() == 0` on **every game** (FO3, FNV, Skyrim SE, FO4 alike).

**Evidence**

`crates/plugin/src/esm/cell/walkers.rs:652-655`:

```rust
} else {
    // Skip other record types (PGRE, PMIS, NAVM, etc.)
    reader.skip_record(&header);
}
```

Live parse on `Fallout3.esm`: `navmeshes=0`; same on `FalloutNV.esm`. The single `navi=1` is the NAVI master parsed correctly at the top level. `parse_navm` itself is functional and tested at `records/misc/world.rs:390 (parse_navm_extracts_version)`.

**Impact**

The future AI / pathfinding subsystem has no navmesh data to consume. Cross-game (not FO3-specific) — but visible on FO3 because Fallout 3 ships ~30,000 NAVM records across its cells (per UESP CSWiki). For now this only affects code that hasn't been written yet, hence MEDIUM not HIGH.

**Suggested fix**

In the cell walker's child-record loop (`cell/walkers.rs` before line 652), add an arm that reads NAVM sub-records and routes the resulting `NavmRecord` onto `EsmIndex.navmeshes`. Threading the index map into the cell walker requires either: (a) extend the cell walker signature to take an `&mut HashMap<u32, NavmRecord>`, or (b) defer to a post-pass that re-walks just the cell-NAVM tier. (a) is cleaner and matches how `landscape_textures` is already threaded.

Top-level NAVM dispatch at `records/mod.rs:433` is vestigial — no Bethesda master flattens NAVMs out — but harmless. Worth a comment noting that it exists for non-vanilla mods that might.

### F-FO3-D3-03 — NPC_/CREA SCRI script-attachment field not extracted

- **Severity**: LOW (documented gap)
- **Status**: Existing gap documented at `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/index.rs:504-509`; no GitHub issue captures the FO3 footprint specifically.
- **Location**: `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/actor.rs:445-712` (`parse_npc`)

**Description**

`parse_npc` reads RNAM / CNAM / VTCK / SNAM / CNTO / PKID / DOFT / INAM / TPLT / ACBS / FGGS / FGGA / FGTS / HCLR / HNAM / LNAM / ENAM / PNAM / face-morph block (FO4+) etc. — but no `SCRI` arm. `EsmIndex::base_record_script()` at `index.rs:516-538` walks `activators / containers / terminals / items` (every typed-map record that captures `script_form_id`); NPC_ and CREA are absent because the parser never stores SCRI on them.

**Evidence**

Raw sub-record histogram on Fallout3.esm:

```
[FO3] NPC_=1647 (SCRI on  398 = 24%) | CREA=533 (SCRI on 148 = 27%) | ACTI=774 (SCRI on 616 = 79%)
[FNV] NPC_=3816 (SCRI on 1046 = 27%) | CREA=1578 (SCRI on 260 = 16%) | ACTI=1143 (SCRI on 992 = 86%)
```

ACTI captures SCRI correctly; NPC_ and CREA do not. 24-27% of FO3 actors author an attached script that the parser silently drops.

**Impact**

Cannot resolve NPC-attached SCPT references at runtime. Consumer-side this manifests when the M47.0 actor AI hooks try to look up `npc.script_form_id` — there is no field. Per-NPC reactive behavior (custom dialogue triggers, faction reactions tied to per-NPC scripts) is unreachable. Half of FO3's named NPCs ship custom logic — Three Dog's broadcast triggers, Moira Brown's questline gates, Megaton settler greetings, etc. Same impact on FNV's 1,046 SCRI-bearing NPCs.

**Suggested fix**

Add `pub script_form_id: u32` to `NpcRecord` at `crates/plugin/src/esm/records/actor.rs:~125`, then add an arm in `parse_npc` near the other sub-record arms:

```rust
b"SCRI" if sub.data.len() >= 4 => {
    record.script_form_id = SubReader::new(&sub.data).u32_or_default();
}
```

Also extend `EsmIndex::base_record_script()` to walk `npcs` and `creatures` (`index.rs:524-537`). The coverage-gap comment at `index.rs:506-509` is the doc to update once shipped. Belongs to M47.0 actor-AI follow-ups, not this audit's fix bundle.

## Methodology

1. `cargo test -p byroredux-plugin --test parse_real_esm parse_rate_fo3_esm -- --ignored --nocapture` — confirms `total=37459`, zero errors at `RUST_LOG=trace`.
2. Ad-hoc category-breakdown example (kept under `/tmp/audit/fo3/`) dumps every category count for both ESMs.
3. Ad-hoc top-level GRUP enumerator walks every label in both ESMs and diffs against the dispatch arms in `records/mod.rs` — zero FO3 labels miss dispatch.
4. Ad-hoc WATR sub-record histogrammer walks every WATR record's sub-records on both ESMs — surfaces the NNAM/TNAM split.
5. Ad-hoc NPC_/CREA/ACTI SCRI histogrammer quantifies the documented gap.
